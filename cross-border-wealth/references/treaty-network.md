# Bilateral Tax Treaty Network — WHT Rate Matrix & Structuring Notes

> **Status**: `[model knowledge — verify]` throughout unless otherwise noted.
> All withholding tax (WHT) rates reflect treaty rates for qualifying recipients
> (i.e., beneficial owners that satisfy LOB/PPT requirements).
> **Rates must be verified via official treaty texts, IBFD, or jurisdiction
> revenue-authority publications before any reliance in structuring.**
> Domestic anti-avoidance rules, GAAR, substance requirements, and beneficial
> ownership tests may override treaty rates even where a treaty is in force.

## 4. Key Treaty Provisions for Structuring

### 4.1 NL-SG (Netherlands-Singapore)

- **Treaty**: In force; revised protocol effective.
- **Dividends**: 0% (qualifying 10%+ holding); 15% portfolio.
- **Interest**: 0%.
- **Royalties**: 0%.
- **LOB**: No comprehensive LOB clause in treaty text.
- **MLI/PPT**: Both NL and SG are MLI signatories. PPT applies to this treaty.
  Singapore elected the PPT-only option. Netherlands adopted PPT.
- **Structuring note**: Singapore holding companies routing dividends to NL
  benefit from 0% WHT but must demonstrate substance in SG and satisfy PPT.
  Dutch conditional WHT on dividends (2024+) may apply on outbound flows to
  low-tax jurisdictions.

### 4.2 LU-US (Luxembourg-United States)

- **Treaty**: 1996 treaty, amended by 2009 protocol.
- **Dividends**: 5% (10%+ holding); 15% portfolio. 0% for 80%+ holding by
  qualifying company subject to LOB.
- **Interest**: 0%.
- **Royalties**: 0%.
- **LOB**: **Comprehensive LOB article** (one of the most detailed in the US
  treaty network). Derivative benefits test, active trade or business test,
  publicly traded test, ownership/base erosion test all apply.
- **PPT**: US is not an MLI signatory. No PPT overlay on US treaties.
- **Structuring note**: LOB is the primary gatekeeper. Luxembourg companies
  must independently qualify under one of the LOB tests. Conduit arrangements
  fail the active trade or business test unless the LU entity has genuine
  economic activity in Luxembourg.

### 4.3 IE-US (Ireland-United States)

- **Treaty**: 1997 treaty, amended by protocol.
- **Dividends**: 5% (10%+ holding); 15% portfolio.
- **Interest**: 0%.
- **Royalties**: 0%.
- **LOB**: **Comprehensive LOB article** (US-style). Similar structure to LU-US.
- **PPT**: No PPT (US not party to MLI).
- **Structuring note**: Ireland's IP regime (Knowledge Development Box) combined
  with 0% royalty WHT to the US is a well-known structure. Irish substance
  requirements have been strengthened post-BEPS. Section 110 SPV rules apply.

### 4.4 SG-HK (Singapore-Hong Kong)

- **Treaty**: In force since 2005.
- **Dividends**: 0% (qualifying holding); 5% portfolio.
- **Interest**: 0%.
- **Royalties**: 5%.
- **LOB**: No LOB clause.
- **MLI/PPT**: Both SG and HK have signed the MLI. PPT applies to this treaty
  pair — both opted for PPT-only approach.
- **Structuring note**: HK does not impose WHT on dividends or interest
  domestically; the treaty mainly benefits SG-source payments. Royalty WHT in
  HK is effectively 4.95% (deemed 30% of income at 16.5%).

### 4.5 NL-UK (Netherlands-United Kingdom)

- **Treaty**: 2008 treaty (replaced 1980 treaty).
- **Dividends**: 0% (qualifying 10%+ holding); 15% portfolio.
- **Interest**: 0%.
- **Royalties**: 0%.
- **LOB**: No comprehensive LOB; main purpose test in treaty.
- **MLI/PPT**: Both are MLI signatories. PPT applies.
- **Structuring note**: Post-Brexit, EU directives no longer apply to NL-UK
  flows — the bilateral treaty is now the sole relief mechanism. UK does not
  impose dividend WHT domestically, so the treaty primarily protects NL-source
  dividend flows.

### 4.6 US-HK (No Treaty)

- **No bilateral tax treaty in force.** US domestic rates apply (30% WHT on
  dividends, interest, royalties from US sources to HK recipients).
- HK does not impose WHT on dividends or interest; outbound payments from HK
  to US are generally not subject to HK WHT.

### 4.7 CH-SG (Switzerland-Singapore)

- **Treaty**: In force.
- **Dividends**: 5% (qualifying holding); 15% portfolio.
- **Interest**: 5%.
- **Royalties**: 5%.
- **LOB**: No comprehensive LOB.
- **MLI/PPT**: PPT applies — both are MLI parties.
- **Structuring note**: Higher treaty rates compared to NL-SG or HK-SG. Swiss
  substance requirements (safe harbour rules) and SG's Section 13 exemptions
  interact with treaty benefits.

### 4.8 NL-JP (Netherlands-Japan)

- **Treaty**: 2010 treaty (replaced 1970 treaty).
- **Dividends**: 0% (50%+ holding for 6 months); 5% (10%+ holding); 10%
  portfolio.
- **Interest**: 0% (financial institutions and government); 10% other.
- **Royalties**: 0%.
- **LOB**: Contains LOB provisions (Japanese treaty practice).
- **MLI/PPT**: Both are MLI signatories. PPT applies.
- **Structuring note**: The 0% dividend rate for 50%+ holdings makes NL
  attractive for Japanese outbound structures. Japan's domestic WHT on
  dividends is 20.42%.

## 5. EU Directives Overlay

Applies to flows **between EU member states** only. As of reference date,
relevant EU members in the matrix: NL, LU, IE, DE.

### 5.1 Parent-Subsidiary Directive (2011/96/EU)

- **Effect**: 0% WHT on dividends paid by a subsidiary in one EU state to a
  parent in another EU state.
- **Conditions**: Parent holds 10%+ of capital for at least 12 consecutive
  months (some states require 24 months). Parent must be in a qualifying legal
  form and subject to corporate tax without exemption.
- **Anti-abuse** (2015 amendment): Member states must deny benefits where
  arrangements are not genuine (i.e., put in place for the main purpose of
  obtaining a tax advantage — mirrors PPT logic).
- **Pairs in matrix**: NL-LU, NL-IE, NL-DE, LU-IE, LU-DE, IE-DE.

### 5.2 Interest & Royalties Directive (2003/49/EC)

- **Effect**: 0% WHT on interest and royalty payments between associated
  companies of different EU member states.
- **Conditions**: 25%+ direct holding (or common 25%+ parent). Recipient must
  be beneficial owner. Qualifying legal forms. Held for uninterrupted period
  (2 years in some implementations).
- **Pairs in matrix**: NL-LU, NL-IE, NL-DE, LU-IE, LU-DE, IE-DE.
- **Note**: Some states (e.g., historically Portugal, Greece) had transitional
  periods. All major jurisdictions in this matrix now fully implement.

### 5.3 Merger Directive (2009/133/EC)

- **Effect**: Tax-neutral cross-border mergers, divisions, transfers of assets,
  and exchanges of shares between EU member state companies.
- **Relevance**: Allows restructuring of EU holding chains without triggering
  capital gains tax. Critical for reorganising NL-LU-IE-DE structures.

### 5.4 Post-Brexit Note

- UK lost EU directive benefits from 1 January 2021. All NL-UK, LU-UK, IE-UK,
  DE-UK flows now rely solely on bilateral treaties.

## 6. MLI Impact Summary

The Multilateral Instrument (OECD MLI, BEPS Action 15) modifies covered tax
agreements. Key impacts on this matrix:

### 6.1 Signatories and Ratification

| Jurisdiction | MLI Signatory | MLI Ratified | PPT Adopted | LOB Adopted |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|-------------|-------------|
| NL           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |
| LU           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |
| SG           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes (only)  | No          |
| HK           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes (only)  | No          |
| IE           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |
| UAE          | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |
| CH           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes + SLOB  | Partial     |
| UK           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |
| US           | **No**       | **No**       | N/A         | Via treaty  |
| AU           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |
| TW           | **No**       | **No**       | N/A         | N/A         |
| JP           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |
| DE           | Yes          | Yes          | Yes         | No          |

> US and TW are **not** MLI signatories. US treaties rely on bilateral LOB
> clauses. Taiwan's limited treaty network is entirely bilateral.

### 6.2 Key Reservations

- **Switzerland**: Adopted the **Simplified LOB (SLOB)** in addition to PPT for
  many treaties. SLOB requires qualified person status (resident, publicly
  traded, or active business) as a threshold test before PPT applies.
- **Singapore**: Reserved on Article 8 (dividend transfer provisions) and
  Article 9 (capital gains on shares from real property). Opted for PPT-only
  (no SLOB).
- **Hong Kong**: PPT-only. Reserved on arbitration.
- **Luxembourg**: Adopted PPT. Reserved on certain savings clauses. Opted in
  to arbitration (Part VI) for some treaties.
- **Australia**: Adopted PPT. Also reserved on Article 12 (artificial avoidance
  of PE through commissionnaire arrangements) — opted for broader PE definition.

### 6.3 Treaties NOT Covered by MLI

- All US bilateral treaties (US is not a signatory).
- All Taiwan bilateral treaties (TW is not a signatory).
- Any treaty pair where either party has listed the treaty as not covered in
  their MLI positions. Check the OECD MLI matching database for current
  coverage.

### 6.4 Practical Impact on Structuring

1. **PPT is now the default anti-avoidance test** for nearly all non-US treaty
   pairs in this matrix. Structures must demonstrate that obtaining treaty
   benefits was not one of the principal purposes of the arrangement.
2. **Substance requirements** are effectively mandated by PPT. Shell companies
   with no employees, no office, and no decision-making in the treaty
   jurisdiction will fail PPT.
3. **Transparent entities**: MLI Article 3 (transparent entities) may affect
   partnership and trust structures. NL and LU have both opted in.
4. **PE avoidance**: MLI Article 12-15 modifications broaden PE definitions in
   some treaty pairs (relevant for commissionnaire and similar arrangements).

## Notes for Users

1. **Verify all rates** against current treaty texts. Treaties are regularly
   renegotiated and protocols may alter rates after this document was prepared.
2. **Beneficial ownership** is a threshold requirement in virtually all treaties.
   Conduit entities that lack beneficial ownership status cannot claim treaty
   benefits regardless of the rate shown.
3. **Domestic law overrides**: Some jurisdictions (e.g., NL conditional WHT, AU
   MAAL/DPT) have domestic anti-avoidance rules that operate independently of
   treaty provisions.
4. **OECD Pillar Two** (GloBE rules, 15% minimum tax): From 2024+, Pillar Two
   may reduce the attractiveness of low-WHT treaty routes if the effective tax
   rate in the recipient jurisdiction falls below 15%. This does not change WHT
   rates but affects the overall tax cost of structures.
5. **Taiwan treaties** are typically styled as "agreements" or "arrangements"
   due to political sensitivities and may have different enforcement mechanisms.
