# Jurisdiction Profiles — Quick Reference

Common structuring jurisdictions and their key properties. This is a starting reference — always verify current data via web search before relying on any rate or rule.

All data tagged `[model knowledge — verify]` unless otherwise noted.

## Holding Company Jurisdictions

### Netherlands
- Corporate tax: 25.8% (19% on first €200K)
- Participation exemption: yes (≥5% shareholding)
- WHT on dividends: 15% (0% under EU PSD; reduced under treaties)
- Treaty network: 90+ treaties
- Substance: Dutch substance requirements (payroll, office, qualified directors)
- Pillar Two: implementing QDMTT and IIR
- Note: innovation box (9% on qualifying IP income)

### Luxembourg
- Corporate tax: ~23.87% combined in Luxembourg City (CIT 16% + municipal business tax + solidarity surcharge; CIT cut from 17% to 16% effective Jan 2025)
- Participation exemption: yes (≥10% or €1.2M acquisition price, held ≥12 months)
- SOPARFI regime for holding companies
- Treaty network: 100+ treaties (103 as of 2025)
- Substance: minimum substance under ATAD
- Note: strong IP regime, securitization vehicles, investment fund hub

### Singapore
- Corporate tax: 17% headline
- Capital gains exemption: s13F/13Z (≥20% ordinary shares, held ≥24 months; formerly s13W)
- Foreign dividend exemption: s13(8) (underlying tax ≥15%, subject to conditions)
- WHT on dividends: 0% (one-tier system)
- Treaty network: ~100 treaties (98 full DTAs)
- Substance: real employees, real office, decisions made in SG
- Note: VCC for fund structures, no capital gains tax, territorial system evolving

### Hong Kong
- Corporate tax: 16.5% (8.25% on first HK$2M)
- Territorial system: only HK-sourced profits taxed
- WHT on dividends: 0%
- Treaty network: 55+ treaties (57 signed CDTAs as of 2026)
- No capital gains tax
- Note: new FSIE regime (2023+) requires substance for offshore income exemption

### Ireland
- Corporate tax: 12.5% headline trading rate (15% minimum effective rate applies only to in-scope MNEs with ≥€750M revenue via QDMTT/IIR top-up)
- Knowledge Development Box: 10% on qualifying IP
- Participation exemption: expanded from Jan 2025 — full exemption for qualifying foreign dividends (previously limited to EU/treaty dividends)
- Treaty network: 75+ treaties (78 signed)
- Note: substantial tech/pharma presence; OECD/EU scrutiny post-Apple

### United Arab Emirates (UAE)
- Corporate tax: 9% (introduced 2023, 0% on first AED 375K)
- Free zones: 0% CIT with substance (qualifying income)
- WHT: 0%
- Treaty network: 100+ treaties
- No personal income tax
- Note: substance requirements in free zones; qualifying income definition matters

### Switzerland
- Corporate tax: ~12%–21% (varies by canton; Zug ~11.85%, Bern ~20.5%)
- Participation exemption: yes (≥10% or CHF 1M, ≥1 year)
- Patent box: cantonal, up to 90% reduction
- Treaty network: 100+ treaties
- Note: principal company structures common; OECD scrutiny on TP

## Trust & Foundation Jurisdictions

### British Virgin Islands (BVI)
- No income tax, no capital gains, no estate tax
- VISTA trusts (Virgin Islands Special Trusts Act)
- Economic Substance Act applies to certain activities
- Confidential: no public UBO register (restricted access)

### Cayman Islands
- No direct taxation
- STAR trusts (Special Trusts Alternative Regime)
- Economic Substance Act (2019)
- VCC equivalent: Segregated Portfolio Company

### Jersey
- 0% corporate tax (10% for financial services, 20% for utilities/property)
- Strong trust law (Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984, amended)
- Foundations (Jersey) Law 2009
- Substance requirements for tax residence

### Liechtenstein
- Corporate tax: 12.5%
- Private foundations (Stiftung) — dual purpose (commercial + family)
- Trust-like structures (Treuhänderschaft)
- EEA member (EU access without EU membership)

### Singapore (for trusts)
- No estate duty (abolished 2008)
- Trust law based on English common law
- VCC for fund/family office structures
- Section 13O/13U tax incentives for qualifying funds

### South Dakota (US)
- No state income tax
- Dynasty trusts (no rule against perpetuities)
- Directed trusts (trust adviser model)
- Privacy: no disclosure requirements beyond federal
