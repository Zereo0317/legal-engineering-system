## 1. What "Substance" Means Post-BEPS

Substance is the requirement that an entity demonstrate genuine economic activity in the jurisdiction where it claims tax residence. Three overlapping frameworks drive this:

- **BEPS Action 5 (OECD, 2015)**: Introduced the "substantial activities" requirement for preferential tax regimes. Jurisdictions must show that entities benefiting from low-tax regimes have real operations — not just a brass plate.
- **EU ATAD III / Unshell Directive (proposed 2021, evolving)**: Targets "shell" entities within the EU that lack minimum substance. Imposes gateway tests (income, staff, premises) and denies treaty/directive benefits to entities that fail.
- **Local Economic Substance Acts**: Jurisdictions such as BVI, Cayman, Jersey, Guernsey, and Bermuda enacted domestic substance legislation (2018-2019) under OECD/EU pressure, requiring entities conducting "relevant activities" to demonstrate adequate substance.

**Why it matters for structuring**: An entity without adequate substance risks (a) denial of tax treaty benefits, (b) recharacterisation of income by the parent jurisdiction, (c) fines and strike-off in the entity jurisdiction, and (d) spontaneous exchange of information with other tax authorities.

## 2. Per-Jurisdiction Substance Requirements

### 2.1 Netherlands

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Dutch Fiscal Unity / Withholding Tax Act 2021; Dutch substance requirements (Decree of 2014, updated 2022); ATAD implementation |
| **Applicable activities** | All entities claiming treaty benefits or participation exemption, especially holding and financing companies |
| **Minimum requirements** | At least 50% of board members resident in NL; board decisions made in NL; qualified employees performing CIGA; own office space (not virtual); NL bank account; bookkeeping in NL; minimum EUR 100k annual salary costs |
| **Penalties** | Denial of withholding tax exemption; exchange of information under DAC6/CRS; potential refusal of advance tax ruling |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: 1-2 NL-resident directors, dedicated office (shared acceptable), local admin staff. *IP company*: full-time employees controlling IP decisions, development oversight from NL |
| **Cost estimate** | EUR 150k-300k/year (1-2 employees, office, director fees, admin) |

### 2.2 Luxembourg

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Luxembourg Income Tax Law; Circular LIR 164/2 (transfer pricing); ATAD implementation law of 21 Dec 2018; EU anti-abuse provisions |
| **Applicable activities** | SPVs, holding companies (SOPARFI), securitisation vehicles, IP holding, financing |
| **Minimum requirements** | Luxembourg-resident directors (majority recommended); physical office; qualified staff or outsourced management with demonstrable control; CIGA performed locally; adequate capitalisation |
| **Penalties** | Denial of participation exemption or treaty benefits; tax reassessment; DAC6 reporting triggers |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: 2-3 LU-resident directors, shared office with dedicated desk, 1 local employee for admin. *Finance company*: must show independent decision-making, risk management capacity |
| **Cost estimate** | EUR 120k-250k/year (director fees EUR 30-60k, office EUR 20-40k, 1 employee EUR 60-80k, admin/audit EUR 20-40k) |

### 2.3 Singapore

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Income Tax Act (Section 13O/13U fund incentives); Economic Expansion Incentives Act; Singapore tax residency rules (control and management test) |
| **Applicable activities** | Fund management (13O/13U), regional headquarters, IP holding, treasury centres |
| **Minimum requirements** | Board meetings held in Singapore; at least 1 SG-resident director; local employees managing the entity; physical office; bank accounts in SG; CIGA in SG (investment decisions for funds, management decisions for HoldCos) |
| **Penalties** | Loss of tax incentive (13O/13U); reclassification as non-resident; IRAS audit adjustments |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: 1-2 SG-resident directors, 1 local employee, registered office. *Fund vehicle (13O/13U)*: Singapore-based fund manager with licensed staff, minimum AUM thresholds (SGD 20M for 13O, SGD 50M for 13U), local investment professionals |
| **Cost estimate** | SGD 200k-400k/year (employee SGD 80-150k, office SGD 30-60k, director fees SGD 20-40k, compliance SGD 30-50k) |

### 2.4 Hong Kong (New FSIE Regime 2023+)

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Inland Revenue (Amendment) (Taxation on Specified Foreign-sourced Income) Ordinance 2022 (effective 1 Jan 2023); expanded 2024 to cover disposal gains on assets other than shares |
| **Applicable activities** | Entities receiving foreign-sourced dividends, interest, IP income, or disposal gains. MNE entities as defined under the regime |
| **Minimum requirements** | **Economic substance test**: adequate number of qualified employees, adequate operating expenditure, adequate office space — all in HK. **Participation exemption route** (for dividends/gains): 5% holding for 12 months + economic substance OR holding is pure equity and no deduction claimed. IP income requires the nexus approach |
| **Penalties** | Foreign-sourced income deemed taxable in HK (8.25-16.5% profits tax) if substance test failed; no separate administrative penalty but tax cost is the penalty |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: demonstrate HK-based decision-making on investments, 1-2 local directors, office, local staff. Many structures now use the participation exemption route to avoid the substance test entirely where possible |
| **Cost estimate** | HKD 600k-1.2M/year (1-2 employees HKD 300-600k, office HKD 120-240k, directors HKD 100-200k, compliance HKD 80-150k) |

### 2.5 Ireland

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Taxes Consolidation Act 1997 (s.23A — central management and control); Finance Act 2014 (eliminated "double Irish"); ATAD implementation via Finance Act 2019 |
| **Applicable activities** | IP-intensive companies, treasury/financing, group holding, trading operations |
| **Minimum requirements** | Irish-resident directors (at least 1 required by Companies Act 2014); board meetings in Ireland; office premises; employees with relevant expertise; strategic decisions made in Ireland |
| **Penalties** | Denial of 12.5% trading rate (recharacterised as 25% passive); denial of treaty benefits; Revenue audit and reassessment |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: 2 Irish-resident directors, local office, 1-2 staff. *IP company*: requires significant Irish-based R&D or management of IP; KDB (Knowledge Development Box) requires nexus approach compliance |
| **Cost estimate** | EUR 180k-350k/year (employees EUR 80-150k, directors EUR 30-60k, office EUR 25-50k, compliance EUR 30-50k) |

### 2.6 UAE (Free Zone Substance Rules)

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022 (Corporate Tax Law); Ministerial Decision No. 265 of 2023 (Qualifying Free Zone Person rules); Cabinet Decision No. 55 of 2023 |
| **Applicable activities** | Entities in qualifying free zones seeking 0% rate on "qualifying income" — manufacturing, processing, holding of shares, treasury/financing, HQ services, shipping, fund management, wealth/asset management |
| **Minimum requirements** | Adequate substance in the free zone: employees, assets, and expenditure proportionate to activities; core income-generating activities in the free zone; own office (not virtual); maintain books and records in the UAE; annual audited financial statements |
| **Penalties** | Loss of 0% free zone rate — standard 9% CT applies; cannot retroactively elect back in for 4 tax periods; penalties under tax procedures law for non-compliance |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: 1-2 UAE-based employees, physical office in the free zone (e.g., ADGM, DIFC, DMCC), local directors, investment decision-making in UAE. *Trading company*: significant employees, contracts executed from free zone |
| **Cost estimate** | USD 100k-250k/year (free zone license USD 10-30k, office USD 15-50k, 1-2 employees USD 40-120k, visa costs USD 5-15k, audit/compliance USD 15-30k) |

### 2.7 Switzerland

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Federal Act on Tax Reform and AHV Financing (TRAF, 2020); Cantonal tax laws; Swiss-EU mutual recognition of substance standards |
| **Applicable activities** | Holding companies, principal structures, IP-intensive companies, finance companies, trading companies |
| **Minimum requirements** | Swiss-based management (directors, C-suite); office space; employees performing key functions; CIGA in Switzerland. Post-TRAF, the old holding/domiciliary/mixed company statuses were abolished; all companies must demonstrate genuine activity for any preferential cantonal rates |
| **Penalties** | Denial of reduced cantonal rate; federal tax reassessment; risk of treaty override by counterpart jurisdictions |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: at least 2/3 purpose must be holding activity; Swiss-resident directors; local admin. *Principal company*: meaningful operational staff, risk-bearing activities in Switzerland. Cantonal competition (Zug, Schwyz, Vaud) still allows effective rates of 11-14% with genuine substance |
| **Cost estimate** | CHF 200k-500k/year (higher than many jurisdictions due to salary levels; 1-2 employees CHF 100-250k, office CHF 30-80k, directors CHF 40-80k, compliance CHF 30-50k) |

### 2.8 BVI (Economic Substance Act 2018)

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Economic Substance (Companies and Limited Partnerships) Act 2018, as amended 2019 and 2020; Economic Substance Code (Revised 2024) |
| **Applicable activities** | 9 "relevant activities": banking, insurance, fund management, finance and leasing, headquarters, shipping, holding, IP, distribution and service centres |
| **Minimum requirements** | **General**: CIGA conducted in BVI; directed and managed in BVI; adequate employees, premises, expenditure in BVI. **Pure equity holding** (reduced test): comply with BVI filing obligations; adequate employees and premises for holding activity (may be registered agent). **IP holding** (enhanced test): high degree of control over IP development/exploitation from BVI; senior employees with necessary skills in BVI |
| **Penalties** | First offence: USD 5,000 fine. Second offence: USD 10,000 fine. Continued failure: struck off register. Spontaneous exchange of information with relevant jurisdictions (parent, UBO, EU) |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company (pure equity)*: registered agent, local director presence, basic office facilities — lightest requirements. *IP company*: extremely difficult to satisfy in BVI; strongly discouraged unless genuine BVI-based IP management team exists |
| **Cost estimate** | USD 25k-80k/year for pure equity holding (registered agent USD 5-10k, director fees USD 10-25k, minimal office USD 5-15k, compliance USD 5-15k). IP or active business: USD 150k+ |

### 2.9 Cayman Islands (International Tax Co-operation Act 2017)

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | International Tax Co-operation (Economic Substance) Act 2018 (as amended); Economic Substance Rules v3.0 (2020); International Tax Co-operation (Economic Substance) (Prescribed Dates) Regulations |
| **Applicable activities** | 9 relevant activities (same categories as BVI): banking, distribution and service centre, financing and leasing, fund management, headquarters, holding, insurance, IP, shipping |
| **Minimum requirements** | CIGA in Cayman; directed and managed in Cayman; adequate employees/premises/expenditure. **Reduced requirements for pure equity holding**: compliance with statutory obligations, adequate human resources and premises (can be outsourced to registered office provider). **Enhanced requirements for IP**: must demonstrate high degree of control in Cayman |
| **Penalties** | Year 1 failure: CI$ 10,000 fine. Year 2 continued failure: CI$ 100,000 fine. Continued failure: strike-off. Spontaneous exchange of substance information to relevant jurisdictions |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: very similar to BVI — registered office, local governance, minimal local staff. *Fund management*: typically met through Cayman-based fund administrators and directors, but requires genuine decision-making presence |
| **Cost estimate** | USD 30k-100k/year for holding (registered office USD 5-15k, director fees USD 15-35k, compliance USD 10-25k). Active business: USD 200k+ |

### 2.10 Jersey

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019; Taxation (Economic Substance — Requirements) (Jersey) Order 2019 |
| **Applicable activities** | Same 9 relevant activities as BVI/Cayman (aligned with OECD/EU framework) |
| **Minimum requirements** | Directed and managed in Jersey; CIGA in Jersey; adequate employees/premises/expenditure. Pure equity holding: reduced test — maintain adequate records and comply with obligations. Jersey benefits from being an established finance centre with deep pool of qualified professionals |
| **Penalties** | Initial: GBP 10,000 fine. Continued: GBP 100,000 fine. Information exchange with relevant jurisdictions. Ultimate sanction: strike-off |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: Jersey-resident directors (readily available), registered office, 1 local employee or outsourced admin. More credible than BVI/Cayman in EU eyes due to AIFMD passporting agreements and long regulatory track record |
| **Cost estimate** | GBP 40k-120k/year (director fees GBP 15-40k, registered office GBP 5-15k, admin/compliance GBP 15-40k, office if needed GBP 10-25k) |

### 2.11 Guernsey

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Income Tax (Substance Requirements) (Guernsey) (Amendment) Ordinance 2018; Substance Requirements Guidance Notes |
| **Applicable activities** | Same 9 relevant activities as Jersey/BVI/Cayman |
| **Minimum requirements** | Directed and managed in Guernsey; CIGA in Guernsey; adequate employees/premises/expenditure. Pure equity holding: reduced test. Guernsey's regime closely mirrors Jersey's |
| **Penalties** | Initial: GBP 10,000. Continued: GBP 100,000. Information exchange. Strike-off |
| **Practical guidance** | Very similar to Jersey. Guernsey has a strong insurance and captive sector. *Holding company*: local directors, registered office, outsourced admin. Slightly smaller professional services pool than Jersey but lower costs in some areas |
| **Cost estimate** | GBP 35k-100k/year (marginally lower than Jersey for equivalent structures) |

### 2.12 Bermuda

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | Economic Substance Act 2018 (as amended); Economic Substance Regulations 2019; Guidance Notes (updated 2021) |
| **Applicable activities** | Banking, insurance, fund management, financing and leasing, headquarters, holding, IP, shipping, distribution and service |
| **Minimum requirements** | CIGA in Bermuda; directed and managed in Bermuda; adequate employees/premises/expenditure. Pure equity holding: reduced test. Bermuda has a deep insurance/reinsurance ecosystem making insurance substance straightforward |
| **Penalties** | First year: BMD 10,000-50,000 (graduated by size). Second year: BMD 50,000-250,000. Continued: strike-off. Information exchange |
| **Practical guidance** | *Holding company*: local directors, registered office, outsourced admin. *Insurance/reinsurance*: Bermuda excels here — substantial local underwriting talent. IP structures: difficult to sustain substance |
| **Cost estimate** | USD 50k-150k/year for holding. Insurance vehicles: significantly higher but offset by available infrastructure |

### 2.13 Liechtenstein

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Legal basis** | EEA membership subjects Liechtenstein to EU anti-abuse directives; Steuergesetz (Tax Act) 2011; Foundation Law (PGR); ATAD-equivalent implementation |
| **Applicable activities** | Foundations (Stiftung), trusts (Treuhänderschaft), holding companies (Anstalt), fund vehicles |
| **Minimum requirements** | Local management body (board or council); registered office; at least basic local administration (typically through a licensed trust company); key decisions made in Liechtenstein |
| **Penalties** | Denial of EEA/Swiss treaty benefits; tax reassessment; regulatory sanctions from FMA (Financial Market Authority) for regulated entities |
| **Practical guidance** | *Foundation*: Liechtenstein trust company acts as council member, provides office and admin. Well-suited for discretionary structures and private wealth. *Holding company*: local board majority, office, basic staff. Advantage: access to EEA treaty network with low 12.5% headline rate |
| **Cost estimate** | CHF 30k-100k/year for foundation (trust company fees CHF 15-40k, regulatory/compliance CHF 10-25k, admin CHF 5-20k). Active company structures: CHF 100k-250k |

## 3. EU ATAD III / Unshell Directive

### Status (as of mid-2025)

The Unshell Directive proposal (COM/2021/565) has been under negotiation since December 2021. Progress has been slow due to unanimity requirements in the EU Council on tax matters. Key developments:

- The European Parliament endorsed an amended version in January 2023
- Council negotiations remain ongoing with no final adoption date confirmed
- Several member states have pushed for modifications or exemptions

### Key Provisions (Based on Proposal and Parliament Amendments)

1. **Gateway test** (all three must be met to trigger scrutiny):
   - More than 75% of revenue is "relevant income" (passive — dividends, interest, royalties, rent, capital gains)
   - More than 60% of relevant income is cross-border (earned or paid across borders)
   - Administration/management of day-to-day operations outsourced in prior 2 years

2. **Minimum substance indicators** (entity must demonstrate all three):
   - Own premises or exclusive-use premises in the member state
   - At least one active EU bank account
   - At least one director who is resident near the entity and qualified to make decisions (or a sufficient number of employees resident near the entity and carrying out CIGA)

3. **Consequences of failing**: Entity is presumed a "shell" and loses access to:
   - Parent-Subsidiary Directive (withholding tax exemptions)
   - Interest and Royalties Directive
   - Tax treaty benefits
   - The entity's member state must issue a tax certificate denoting shell status; counterpart states apply withholding at domestic rates

4. **Exemptions**: Regulated financial entities, listed companies, entities with at least 5 FTE employees conducting CIGA, certain holding companies with same-jurisdiction shareholders

### Impact on Structuring

- Intermediate holding companies in the Netherlands, Luxembourg, Ireland, and Malta with minimal staff and outsourced management are primary targets
- Structures will need to either (a) build up genuine local substance, (b) relocate functions, or (c) simplify the chain to eliminate entities that serve no purpose beyond treaty shopping
- Even before formal adoption, several member states are applying the "spirit" of Unshell in administrative practice and rulings

## 4. Substance Checklist

Use this checklist when assessing whether an entity has adequate substance. Score each item and flag any "No" answers for remediation.

### Governance and Decision-Making

- [ ] Board of directors includes residents of the entity's jurisdiction
- [ ] Board meetings are held in the jurisdiction (at least quarterly)
- [ ] Minutes reflect genuine decision-making (not rubber-stamping)
- [ ] Strategic decisions (investments, disposals, financings) are made locally
- [ ] Directors have relevant qualifications and experience
- [ ] Directors are not overboarded (serving on an excessive number of boards)

### Personnel

- [ ] Entity has at least one full-time employee in the jurisdiction (or equivalent outsourced resource for pure holding)
- [ ] Employees have qualifications relevant to the entity's activities
- [ ] Employment contracts, payroll, and social security contributions are local
- [ ] Key personnel are not simultaneously key personnel of dozens of other entities

### Premises

- [ ] Entity has a physical office (not a virtual office or mail-forwarding address)
- [ ] Office is proportionate to activities (a holding company does not need a floor, but needs more than a nameplate)
- [ ] Lease or ownership documentation is on file
- [ ] Entity's correspondence address matches its actual operating location

### Financial

- [ ] Entity has at least one bank account in the jurisdiction
- [ ] Operating expenditure is incurred locally and is proportionate to revenue/assets
- [ ] Entity is adequately capitalised for its activities
- [ ] Transfer pricing documentation supports arm's-length compensation for substance providers

### Core Income-Generating Activities (CIGA)

- [ ] CIGA for the entity's relevant activity are performed in the jurisdiction
- [ ] For **holding**: taking and implementing decisions on holding/disposing of shares
- [ ] For **financing**: negotiating, agreeing, and managing funding terms
- [ ] For **IP**: control over development, enhancement, maintenance, protection, exploitation of IP
- [ ] For **fund management**: taking decisions on investments, managing risk, executing trades
- [ ] CIGA are documented — not just asserted

### Compliance and Documentation

- [ ] Annual financial statements are prepared and filed locally
- [ ] Entity files required substance declarations/returns
- [ ] Entity maintains a substance file: org chart, employee list, office lease, board minutes, evidence of CIGA
- [ ] Entity has considered CRS/DAC6/FATCA reporting obligations
- [ ] Legal opinions or tax rulings are current and reflect actual (not planned) substance
