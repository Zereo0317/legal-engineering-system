## Overview

Professional counseling licensure in the US is **state-regulated** — there is no federal
license. Each state sets its own requirements. For a foreign-educated professional, this
fragmentation is a feature: some states are significantly more accessible to international
credentials than others.

**Core requirements across all states:**
- Master's degree (48–60 credit hours) in counseling or closely related field
- Supervised clinical experience (1,500–4,500 hours depending on state)
- National exam (NCE and/or NCMHCE)
- Credential evaluation for foreign degrees (NACES or AICE member agency)

## National Exams

### NCE (National Counselor Examination)
- **Format**: 200 multiple-choice, 3 hrs 45 min
- **Content**: 8 CACREP domains
- **Cost**: $275 (2026) [verified 2026-05]
- **Pass rate**: ~70–75% first attempt [model knowledge — verify]
- **Prep time**: 2–4 months typical

### NCMHCE (National Clinical Mental Health Counselor Examination)
- **Format**: 11 clinical case simulations
- **Content**: Assessment/diagnosis, treatment planning, DSM-5
- **Cost**: $275 (2026) [verified 2026-05]
- **Pass rate**: ~65–70% first attempt [model knowledge — verify]
- **Prep time**: 3–6 months typical

## Counseling Compact (Interstate Practice)

As of 2026, three states are operational:
- **Arizona** (operational)
- **Minnesota** (operational)
- **Ohio** (operational January 2026)

The Compact allows licensed counselors to practice across member states without separate
licenses. Strategically: licensure in a Compact state = force multiplier.

### Recommended Pathways

**Strategy 1: Florida Express** (Fastest to independent practice)
- Target: LMHC in Florida (only 1,500 supervised hours)
- Online CACREP Master's + in-person practicum in FL
- Timeline: ~4 years total | Cost: ~$40K–$60K

**Strategy 2: Compact Multiplier** (Best long-term value)
- Target: LPC in Arizona or Ohio (Counseling Compact states)
- License in one → practice in multiple states
- Timeline: ~5 years | Multistate practice from day one

**Strategy 3: Texas Foreign Degree Express**
- Texas BHEC explicitly evaluates foreign degrees
- If foreign Master's obtainable quickly, may bypass US Master's
- Risk: coursework gaps may require remediation

**Strategy 4: Telehealth Arbitrage**
- License in Compact state
- Practice telehealth across state lines
- Serve high-demand markets from lower-cost locations
