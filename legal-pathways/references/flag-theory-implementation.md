## Overview

Flag Theory is the discipline of distributing different aspects of your life across
multiple jurisdictions to optimize for freedom, privacy, tax efficiency, and asset
protection. Originally proposed by Harry D. Schultz (3 flags) and expanded by
W.G. Hill (5 flags), modern practitioners use 6–7 flags.

**Core principle: No single government should have total jurisdiction over your life.**

This is legal optimization, not tax evasion. Every position must be legally defensible
in every jurisdiction it touches. The line is drawn by genuine residence, truthful
disclosure, and compliance with reporting obligations.

## Implementation Architecture

### The Sovereign Individual Stack

```
Citizenship 1 (birth)          Citizenship 2 (CBI/naturalization)
        │                              │
  Tax Residence (zero/low tax jurisdiction)
        │
  Business Entity (optimal jurisdiction)
        │
  ┌─────┴──────┐
  │            │
Banking     Investments       Real Estate
(asset      (brokerage,       (lifestyle,
haven)      crypto)           rental income)
        │
  Insurance/Trust Structure (asset protection)
```

### Compliance Framework

| Obligation | When It Applies | How to Handle |
|---|---|---|
| **CRS/AEOI** | Financial accounts in any CRS jurisdiction | Report to tax residence country automatically |
| **FATCA** | US persons (citizens, GC holders) | Report worldwide income; FBAR for foreign accounts >$10K |
| **Exit tax** | Leaving high-tax jurisdictions (US, France, etc.) | Plan departure; may owe tax on unrealized gains |
| **Controlled Foreign Corporation** | Owning companies in low-tax jurisdictions | Ensure substance; may be taxed in residence country |
| **GAAR** | Any aggressive tax structure | Ensure genuine economic purpose beyond tax |
| **DAC6** | EU mandatory disclosure for cross-border arrangements | Report arrangements with tax hallmarks |

### Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| **Claiming non-residence while spending >183 days** | Tax authority reasserts residence | Track days meticulously; stay under threshold |
| **No genuine economic activity at business address** | GAAR; substance challenge | Maintain real operations and employees |
| **Using CBI passport for banking without disclosing other citizenships** | AML violation; account closure | Disclose all citizenships to all banks |
| **Ignoring CFC rules** | Home country taxes your offshore company's income | Structure with substance; get professional advice |
| **US citizenship renunciation without planning** | Exit tax on worldwide assets at FMV | Plan 5+ years ahead; covered expatriate rules |

## Private Jet Integration

### Aircraft in the Sovereign Structure

The private jet is a module in the sovereign individual stack:

```
Owner (personal tax residence)
     │
  Holding Structure (trust/HoldCo)
     │
  Aircraft SPV (registration jurisdiction)
     │ owns
  Aircraft (registry: IoM / Cayman / Aruba)
     │ managed by
  Management Company (AOC holder)
     │ employs
  Pilots + Crew (employment jurisdiction)
```

### Registration Optimization

| If You're Based In | Register In | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Asia-Pacific | Isle of Man (M) | 0% tax; globally recognized; strong regulation |
| Europe | IoM or San Marino (T7) | Temp admission avoids import VAT |
| Americas | FAA (N) via Montana LLC | No sales tax in MT; FAA is gold standard |
| Middle East | Cayman (VP-C) or Aruba (P4) | 0% tax; flexible ownership |
| Multi-region | Aruba (P4) | No local ownership requirement |

### Pilot Licensing for Owner-Operators

See `aviation-licensing-global.md` for full details. Key points:

1. **PPL + IR + ME + Type Rating** required for jet operations
2. **Total cost**: $45K–$145K depending on aircraft and training path
3. **Cheapest path**: PPL abroad (Philippines/SA) → convert to FAA → type rating in US
4. **Fastest path**: Accelerated US program (4–6 months total)
