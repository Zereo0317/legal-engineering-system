# Aviation Licensing Global — PPL, Type Ratings & Aircraft Registry Engineering

*Legal engineering reference for pilot certification and aircraft ownership optimization.
Treats aviation authorities as jurisdictional building blocks — same principles, different domain.*

## Overview

Flying a private jet requires stacking multiple certifications:

```
PPL (Private Pilot License)
  → IR (Instrument Rating)
    → ME (Multi-Engine Rating)
      → Type Rating (specific aircraft)
        = Authorized to fly your jet
```

Each layer can be obtained in different jurisdictions at different costs and timelines.
The engineering question: which combination of jurisdictions produces the fastest,
cheapest, most globally portable certification stack?

- **Minimum flight hours**: 40 hours (35 on integrated course)

- All ICAO member states must meet or exceed these minimums

## Aircraft Registration Jurisdictions

*Where you register the aircraft is a separate optimization from where you get licensed.
Registration affects taxes, operating flexibility, insurance, and reputation.*

### Comparison Matrix

| Registry | Prefix | Corp Tax | VAT/Import | Insurance Tax | Flexibility | Reputation | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **USA (FAA)** | N | 0–37% | Sales/use tax varies by state | Varies | High | Excellent | US-based operators |
| **Isle of Man** | M | 0% | Temporary admission (deferred) | 0% | High | Excellent | European operations; privacy |
| **Cayman Islands** | VP-C | 0% | 0% | 0% | High | Good | Offshore ownership; leasing |
| **San Marino** | T7 | Low | Favorable import regime | Low | High | Good | EU operations; flexible ownership |
| **Aruba** | P4 | 0% | 0% | 0% | High | Good | International operations; no local ownership required |
| **Bermuda** | VP-B | 0% | 0% | 0% | Moderate | Good | Charter operations |
| **Malta** | 9H | Low | EU import duty applies | EU standard | Moderate | Good | EU-based operators |
| **UK** | G | 0–25% | 20% VAT (but temp admission) | 6% IPT | High | Excellent | UK-based operators |

### Strategic Registration Decisions

**For a non-US owner flying primarily in Asia-Pacific:**
- **Isle of Man (M)**: Best combination of tax efficiency, regulatory quality, and international acceptance
- **Cayman (VP-C)**: Zero tax, good for complex ownership structures
- **Aruba (P4)**: No local ownership requirement, ideal for multinational structures

**For a US-based owner:**
- **FAA (N)**: Required for domestic operations; use Delaware LLC for asset protection
- **Montana LLC**: No sales tax on aircraft purchase
- **Oregon**: No sales tax state

**For an owner operating primarily in Europe:**
- **Isle of Man (M)**: Pioneer registry for European private aviation; temporary admission scheme avoids VAT
- **San Marino (T7)**: Flexible; accepts non-EU ownership structures
- **Malta (9H)**: If EU membership is strategically important

## The Private Jet Ownership Stack (Legal Engineering View)

```
Owner (personal tax residence jurisdiction)
     │
  Holding Company (tax-efficient jurisdiction)
     │ 100% ownership
  Aircraft SPV (registration jurisdiction)
     │ owns
  Aircraft (registered in optimal registry)
     │ managed by
  Management Company (operating certificate)
     │ employs
  Pilots (licensed in aircraft state of registry)
```

### Common Structures

| Structure | Tax Efficiency | Asset Protection | Privacy | Complexity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Direct personal ownership | 🔴 Low | 🔴 None | 🔴 None | 🟢 Simple |
| Single LLC ownership | 🟡 Moderate | 🟡 Some | 🟡 Some | 🟢 Simple |
| Holding → SPV → Aircraft | 🟢 High | 🟢 High | 🟢 High | 🟡 Moderate |
| Trust → HoldCo → SPV → Aircraft | 🟢 High | 🟢 Maximum | 🟢 Maximum | 🔴 Complex |
| Fractional (1/8–1/16 share) | 🟡 Moderate | 🟡 Some | 🟡 Some | 🟢 Simple |

### Fractional Ownership Tax Benefits (US)

- **Section 168(k) Bonus Depreciation**: Phasing down — 40% in 2025, 20% in 2026 (was 100% through 2022; Congress may restore) [model knowledge — verify]
- **Section 179 Expensing**: Immediate deduction of aircraft cost up to limit
- **Operating cost deductions**: Management fees, occupied hourly fees, maintenance
- **Key condition**: Must demonstrate "qualified business use" (>50% business travel)

## License Conversion Pathways

*Got a license in one country? Here's how to convert it to another.*

| From → To | Process | Additional Requirements | Cost | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SACAA (South Africa) → FAA | Verification + FAA written exam | TSA security check; FAA medical | $500–$1,000 | 2–4 months |
| CAAP (Philippines) → FAA | Verification + FAA checkride | Must hold current foreign license | $1,000–$2,000 | 2–4 months |
| FAA → EASA | Theory exam (7 subjects) + skill test | EASA medical; language proficiency | $5,000–$10,000 | 3–6 months |
| EASA → FAA | FAA written exam + checkride | TSA security check; FAA medical | $1,000–$2,000 | 2–4 months |
| FAA → CAAS (Singapore) | Skill test + theory exam | CAAS medical | $2,000–$5,000 | 2–4 months |
| Any ICAO → FAA (Part 61.75) | Foreign license validation | Valid foreign license + FAA medical | $500–$1,000 | 1–3 months |

### Sovereign Pilot Strategy

The optimal path for a globally mobile individual:

1. **Get PPL in cheapest ICAO jurisdiction** (Philippines: $5K–8K, South Africa: $8K–12K)
2. **Convert to FAA** (Part 61.75 validation, ~$1K)
3. **Add IR + ME under FAA** ($15K–$23K)
4. **Get type rating at FlightSafety/SimCom** ($18K–$100K depending on aircraft)
5. **Register aircraft in optimal jurisdiction** (IoM, Cayman, or Aruba)
6. **Structure ownership through tax-efficient SPV**

**Total: $45K–$145K** depending on aircraft class (vs. $70K–$220K if done entirely in the US)

## Medical Certificate Requirements

| Authority | Class | Requirements | Validity |
|---|---|---|---|
| FAA | Third Class (PPL) | Vision, hearing, general health | 60 months (<40) / 24 months (≥40) |
| FAA | Second Class (commercial) | More stringent vision/cardio | 12 months |
| FAA | First Class (ATP) | Most stringent | 12 months (<40) / 6 months (≥40) |
| EASA | Class 2 (PPL) | Similar to FAA Third Class | 60 months (<40) / 24 months (≥40) |
| FAA BasicMed | Alternative to Third Class | Online course + physician exam | 48 months |

**BasicMed advantage**: If you fly aircraft under 6,000 lbs, up to 5 passengers, under
18,000 ft, at speeds up to 250 knots, you can use BasicMed instead of a traditional
FAA medical — much simpler to obtain.
