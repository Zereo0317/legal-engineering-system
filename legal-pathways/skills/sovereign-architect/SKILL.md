# /sovereign-architect

Designs the complete sovereign individual architecture — treating the world's jurisdictions as building blocks for personal freedom, asset protection, tax optimization, and global mobility. Integrates all other legal-pathways skills into a unified strategic design.

## Instructions

1. **Load pathway profile.** Read `~/.claude/plugins/config/legal-engineering/legal-pathways/CLAUDE.md`. If not populated, suggest running `/legal-pathways:cold-start-interview`.

   Also load cross-border wealth profile if available:
   `~/.claude/plugins/config/legal-engineering/cross-border-wealth/CLAUDE.md`

2. **Clarify the scope.** From $ARGUMENTS:
   - **Full sovereign design**: Design all 7 flags from scratch
   - **Specific flag**: Deep dive on one flag (citizenship, tax residence, business base, etc.)
   - **Plan B design**: Emergency exit / geopolitical hedge design
   - **Current audit**: Assess existing multi-jurisdiction setup for gaps and risks
   - **Integration**: Connect immigration/credential/aviation plans into unified strategy

3. **Load reference data:**
   - `legal-pathways/references/flag-theory-implementation.md`
   - `legal-pathways/references/citizenship-investment-matrix.md`
   - `legal-pathways/references/singapore-immigration.md`
   - `legal-pathways/references/aviation-licensing-global.md`
   - Cross-border wealth references if applicable:
     - `cross-border-wealth/references/jurisdiction-profiles.md`
     - `cross-border-wealth/references/treaty-network.md`
     - `cross-border-wealth/references/substance-requirements.md`

4. **For each flag, assess and recommend:**

   ### Flag 1: Citizenship Portfolio
   - Current citizenship(s) and passport strength
   - Target additional citizenships (CBI, naturalization, ancestry)
   - Cost-benefit analysis for each option
   - Dual citizenship rules in current and target countries
   - **Critical**: Capture enforcement reality, not just official policy
     - Singapore: officially no dual citizenship, but children born abroad have legal dual until age 22; Article 134 uses "may" not "must"
     - Taiwan: allows dual citizenship (no renunciation required for naturalized citizens since 2016 law change for senior professionals)
     - US: allows dual citizenship but worldwide taxation applies regardless
   - E-2/E-1 treaty implications (Grenada, Turkey for US access)
   - Generational planning: which citizenships benefit children?

   ### Flag 2: Tax Residence
   - Current tax residence and obligations
   - Target tax residence (zero/low/territorial)
   - Exit tax analysis from current residence
   - Physical presence requirements to establish and maintain
   - CRS/AEOI reporting implications
   - CFC rules that follow you to new residence
   - **Connect to**: `/cross-border-wealth:exit-analysis` for tax impact

   ### Flag 3: Business Base
   - Current business jurisdiction and structure
   - Optimal jurisdiction for operations, IP, treasury
   - Corporate tax rate optimization
   - Banking access and payment infrastructure
   - Employee hiring flexibility
   - **Connect to**: `/cross-border-wealth:entity-optimizer`

   ### Flag 4: Asset Haven
   - Current asset locations and structures
   - Optimal jurisdictions for wealth storage
   - Trust/foundation structures for asset protection
   - Banking relationships strategy (multi-jurisdiction)
   - Real estate holdings optimization
   - Crypto/digital asset custody
   - **Connect to**: `/cross-border-wealth:structure-analysis`

   ### Flag 5: Lifestyle Base
   - Where you actually want to live
   - Quality of life factors (healthcare, education, safety, culture)
   - Climate and timezone preferences
   - Visa/residence options for chosen lifestyle base
   - Tax implications of physical presence
   - Cost of living optimization

   ### Flag 6: Digital Infrastructure
   - Digital business operations base
   - Data privacy jurisdiction
   - E-residency programs (Estonia, etc.)
   - Digital banking and fintech access
   - Internet infrastructure quality

   ### Flag 7: Aviation & Mobility
   - Private aviation needs (own vs. charter vs. fractional)
   - Aircraft registration jurisdiction optimization
   - Pilot licensing pathway (if owner-operated)
   - Aircraft ownership structure (SPV, trust, LLC)
   - **Connect to**: `/legal-pathways:aviation-licensing`

   - Dual citizenship prohibitions that force a choice

   - Exit taxes triggered by residence change

## Verification

All jurisdictional data is verified via web search against official government sources.
Dual citizenship rules are verified at both the official policy level AND the practical
enforcement level — the gap between law-on-the-books and law-in-practice is itself a
data point. CBI program costs are verified against program authority publications.
Tax rates are verified against current tax authority guidance.
